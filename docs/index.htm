Electric buses to hit Somerset roads in April

The first wave of electric buses are scheduled to start operating on Somerset bus routes in April.

Work to install electric charging points at Minehead outstation have been completed, with 4 Heliox Rapid 150kW charging units being available for up to 8 buses to charge at once. These buses will begin serving the 28 routes in July, which includes the 28, 28A and X28. This route will be operated by a fleet of 9 Wrightbus GB Kite single deck buses, which have a range of 200+ miles on a single charge.

An additional 10 chargers are currently being installed at the Taunton bus depot on Hamilton Road, allowing for up to 20 buses to charge at once. The Taunton depot will house 16 Yutong buses, 12 of which will be double decked. These buses should start serving routes 1, 2, 21 and 22 in April.

First Buses of Somerset pull out of Taunton Park and Ride Contract.

First Buses of Somerset have informed Somerset Council that they intend to pull out of the local contract to operate the Taunton Park and Ride service, which runs buses back and forth between the Silk Mills and Gateway car parks.

The company claim operating the service is no longer viable for them from a business perspective.

First Buses of Somerset have operated the Taunton Park & Ride since 2014, when they became the interim contractor after WebberBus lost the contract due to complaints about the service. First Buses of Somerset later became the permanent operator of the service.

Somerset Council put the contract out to tender, and awarded the contract to Stagecoach South West, who it's believed will operate the service with buses based at their Tiverton depot.

Posted on 10th December 2023 by TfS.
